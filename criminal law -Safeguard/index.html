Criminal Laws (IPC & CrPC)

×

Criminal offenses

Offenses like theft, assault, fraud, defamation under the Indian Penal Code (IPC) can lead to arrest, jail time, and fines. These charges require immediate legal attention.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Know your rights if arrested (right to remain silent, right to an attorney)
  • Document everything related to the incident
  • Never resist arrest but assert your right to legal counsel
  • Don't sign any documents without legal advice
  • Collect evidence and witness statements that support your case
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can represent you during police questioning, file for bail, challenge illegal detention, prepare a strong defense strategy, cross-examine witnesses, and negotiate for reduced charges or settlements where applicable.

Cybercrimes

Online harassment, hacking, spreading fake news, or financial fraud are regulated under the IT Act. These offenses carry serious penalties including imprisonment. .

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Maintain digital hygiene (strong passwords, updated software)
  • Document all evidence of online harassment or fraud
  • Report incidents to cybercrime authorities promptly
  • Be cautious about information shared online
  • Keep records of all digital communications
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate specializing in cyber law can help you file proper complaints with cybercrime cells, obtain restraining orders against harassers, assist in removing defamatory content, and build a technical defense with expert witnesses.

Traffic violations

Drunk driving, overspeeding, rash driving, or driving without a license can result in heavy fines, license suspension, or even imprisonment for serious cases. .

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Always carry valid driving documents
  • Document all evidence of online harassment or fraud
  • Follow traffic rules strictly
  • Install dashcams to document incidents
  • Never drive under the influence
  • Cooperate with officers but know your rights
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can contest inappropriate charges, negotiate for reduction of penalties, represent you in Motor Vehicle Act cases, and help with license restoration proceedings.

Drug-related Offenses

Possession, consumption, or distribution of illegal drugs under the NDPS Act carries severe penalties including long jail terms.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Be aware of what substances are controlled under the NDPS Act
  • Never carry substances for others
  • Know the difference between quantities classified as 'small' vs 'commercial'
  • Be aware of your rights regarding search and seizure
  • Insist on proper documentation during any search procedures
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can ensure proper legal procedures were followed during search and seizure, contest quantity determinations that affect sentencing, file for bail where applicable, and prepare technical defenses based on forensic evidence.

Civil Laws

×

Defamation (Sec 499 & 500 IPC)

Making false statements that harm someone's reputation, whether spoken (slander) or written (libel), can lead to civil damages or criminal penalties.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Be careful about public statements regarding individuals or organizations
  • Ensure facts are verified before making public accusations
  • Keep records of evidence supporting any statements you make publicly
  • Understand that opinions are generally protected but presented facts must be true
  • Be particularly careful on social media platforms
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can defend you by establishing truth as a defense, proving fair comment, demonstrating lack of harm to reputation, or negotiating settlements outside court to minimize publicity.

Contract Disputes

Breach of agreements, unpaid dues, or violations of legal contracts can lead to civil litigation and financial damages.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Always get agreements in writing with clear terms
  • Keep thorough records of all communications
  • Document any breaches with evidence
  • Send formal notices before escalating to legal action
  • Consider alternative dispute resolution methods before litigation
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can review contracts for enforceability, draft notices for breach, represent you in negotiations, prepare for litigation if necessary, and advise on the most cost-effective resolution strategies.

Property Disputes

Illegal possession, land encroachments, or landlord-tenant conflicts involve complex property laws and can result in prolonged litigation.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Ensure all property documents are properly registered
  • Conduct thorough title verification before purchase
  • Maintain boundary markers and document encroachments
  • Follow proper legal procedures for evictions or possession
  • Keep all tax receipts and ownership documents safely
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can file appropriate suits for possession, represent you in eviction proceedings, negotiate settlements, handle title disputes, and obtain injunctions against illegal construction or encroachment.

Consumer Rights Violations

Fraud by businesses, misleading advertisements, or defective products are covered under the Consumer Protection Act, providing remedies to consumers.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Keep all receipts, warranties, and proof of purchase
  • Document defects or issues with products/services
  • Communicate complaints in writing to businesses first
  • Take photos/videos as evidence where relevant
  • File complaints with consumer forums within limitation periods
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can file consumer complaints in appropriate forums, calculate proper compensation claims, represent you in consumer courts, negotiate with businesses for settlements, and appeal adverse decisions if necessary..

Financial & Tax Laws

×

Income Tax Evasion

Non-payment or misreporting of income can lead to penalties, prosecution, and even imprisonment under the Income Tax Act.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Maintain proper financial records for at least 7 years
  • File returns accurately and on time even if no tax is due
  • Consult tax professionals for complex transactions
  • Respond promptly to tax notices
  • Declare all sources of income completely
What an Advocate Can Do

A tax advocate can represent you before tax authorities, file appeals against incorrect assessments, negotiate penalty reductions, prepare responses to notices, and advise on legal tax planning strategies.

GST Non-compliance

Businesses failing to comply with GST rules can face penalties, interest charges, and business disruptions.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Register for GST if your business crosses the threshold
  • File returns on time even if nil returns
  • Maintain proper invoices and accounting records
  • Understand which exemptions apply to your business
  • Implement proper accounting systems for GST compliance
What an Advocate Can Do

A tax advocate can help with GST registration, filing appeals against incorrect assessments, representing during GST audits, obtaining refunds, and correcting past filing errors.

Loan Defaults

Banks can take legal action for non-repayment of loans under the SARFAESI Act, potentially seizing assets offered as security.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Communicate with lenders proactively if facing financial difficulties
  • Apply for loan restructuring before defaulting
  • Keep records of all payments and communications
  • Understand the security documents you've signed
  • Respond to all legal notices promptly
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can negotiate with banks for restructuring, challenge improper seizure procedures, file for debt relief in appropriate forums, and represent you in debt recovery tribunals..

Money Laundering

Being unknowingly involved in illegal money transactions can lead to serious charges under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Verify the source of funds in significant transactions
  • Be cautious about becoming a financial signatory for others
  • Understand KYC procedures and their importance
  • Report suspicious transaction requests to authorities
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can establish lack of knowledge/intent in money laundering cases, challenge attachment of property orders, represent during Enforcement Directorate investigations, and build defenses based on procedural violations.

Cyber & Privacy Laws ‚û°

Cyber & Privacy Laws

×

Violation of Privacy

Unauthorized sharing of someone's personal data is regulated under the IT Act and can lead to compensation claims and penalties.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Use strong privacy settings on social platforms
  • Be careful about what personal data you share online
  • Use secure communications for sensitive information
  • Check app permissions before installation
  • Report privacy breaches to proper authorities
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can help file complaints with cyber cells, claim compensation for privacy violations, obtain injunctions against further sharing of private data, and pursue damages against platforms not maintaining adequate security.

Illegal Downloading

Copyright violations due to pirated movies, songs, or software can result in civil damages and, in some cases, criminal penalties.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Use legitimate services for content consumption
  • Understand what constitutes fair use versus infringement
  • Be cautious about sharing copyrighted material
  • Check licenses for software before commercial use
  • Remove infringing content if notified
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can respond to copyright notices, negotiate settlements with rights holders, defend against excessive damage claims, and distinguish between direct infringement and inadvertent sharing.

Spreading Misinformation

Posting fake news or hate speech on social media can violate IT Rules 2021 and lead to account suspension or legal consequences.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Verify information before sharing
  • Be cautious about sharing inflammatory content
  • Understand platform policies on content moderation
  • Cite sources where appropriate
  • Be aware of laws regarding speech that incites violence
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can help contest account suspensions, defend against charges of deliberate misinformation, establish good faith sharing of information, and address platform content decisions.

Employment & Labour Laws ‚û°

Employment & Labour Laws

×

Wrongful Termination

Employers firing without notice or compensation can be sued under various labor laws depending on the nature of employment.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Read and preserve your employment contract
  • Document all performance reviews and feedback
  • Keep records of all communications regarding employment
  • Understand applicable notice periods and termination procedures
  • Request written reasons for termination
What an Advocate Can Do

A labor advocate can challenge terminations in labor courts, negotiate settlements, claim rightful compensation, file for reinstatement where applicable, and address procedural violations in the termination process.

Workplace Harassment

Sexual harassment at work is specifically addressed under the POSH Act, requiring employers to have prevention mechanisms.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Document all incidents with dates, times and details
  • Report to Internal Complaints Committee if available
  • Identify potential witnesses
  • Preserve electronic evidence like emails or messages
  • Understand your company's harassment policy
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can help file proper complaints with Internal Committees, represent during hearings, pursue criminal charges if warranted, claim compensation, and address employer inaction.

Violation of Employment Contracts

Breaking bonds, non-compete clauses, and other contract violations can lead to civil litigation and damages.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Understand all clauses before signing employment contracts
  • Negotiate reasonable terms for bonds and non-competes
  • Document any employer violations of contract terms
  • Seek legal advice before taking actions that might breach contracts
  • Attempt good-faith negotiations before contract breach
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can review the enforceability of restrictive clauses, negotiate releases from unreasonable terms, defend against employer claims, and counter-claim for employer breaches of contract terms.

Family & Marriage Laws ‚û°

Family & Marriage Laws

×

Dowry-related Cases

Giving or taking dowry is illegal under the Dowry Prohibition Act and can result in imprisonment and fines.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Understand the difference between gifts and dowry
  • Document all gifts exchanged during marriage with proper descriptions
  • Avoid making demands for specific items/money
  • Keep witnesses present during gift exchanges
  • Maintain records of all expenses shared between families
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can establish that exchanges were gifts, not dowry, gather evidence of false allegations, prepare defense against misuse of dowry laws, and represent you during investigations.

Domestic Violence

Physical, emotional, economic, or sexual abuse within domestic relationships is punishable under the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Document incidents with dates, times, and details
  • Report serious incidents to police or protection officers
  • Seek medical attention and preserve reports for injuries
  • Identify witnesses who can corroborate claims
  • Keep evidence of threats or abusive communications
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can obtain protection orders, residence orders, or maintenance orders, represent in criminal proceedings, and ensure proper implementation of court directives for protection.

Divorce & Maintenance Issues

Legal battles related to alimony, child custody, or maintenance are governed by personal laws (Hindu Marriage Act, Muslim Personal Law, etc.).

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Document contributions to marriage and family expenses
  • Maintain evidence of parenting involvement for custody cases
  • Keep records of income and assets acquired during marriage
  • Attempt mediation before contentious proceedings
  • Document instances of cruelty or desertion if relevant
What an Advocate Can Do

A family law advocate can negotiate settlements, represent in maintenance proceedings, handle custody disputes, file for restraining orders if needed, and ensure equitable division of assets.

Adultery

Though decriminalized, adultery can still be grounds for divorce and can impact maintenance and custody decisions.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Understand that while not criminal, adultery has civil consequences
  • Be prepared for its impact on divorce proceedings
  • Consider the impact on child custody determinations
  • Be aware that evidence of adultery may be presented in court
  • Seek counseling to address relationship issues
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can advise on how adultery might impact divorce proceedings, help present or defend against such allegations appropriately, and negotiate settlements that account for these factors.

Environmental Laws

Littering & Pollution Violations

Burning waste, illegal waste disposal, or causing environmental harm is regulated under the Environment Protection Act with significant penalties.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Follow proper waste disposal procedures
  • Obtain necessary pollution control approvals for businesses
  • Monitor emissions and discharges from business operations
  • Implement proper environmental management systems
  • Stay updated on changing environmental regulations
What an Advocate Can Do

An environmental advocate can help obtain necessary clearances, defend against violation notices, negotiate with pollution control boards, establish compliance efforts, and appeal excessive penalty determinations.

Illegal Construction

Unauthorized building or zoning law violations can result in demolition orders and financial penalties.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Obtain all necessary building permits before construction
  • Verify zoning regulations for your property
  • Conduct proper boundary verification
  • Follow approved building plans strictly
  • Address notices of violation promptly
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can help regularize minor violations where possible, appeal demolition orders, negotiate timelines for compliance, represent in municipal hearings, and challenge improper classification of structures.

Religious & Public Order

×

Religious Offenses

Hate speech, blasphemy, or inciting communal violence are offenses under Section 153A IPC and can lead to imprisonment.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Be respectful when discussing religious practices
  • Avoid statements that could incite hatred
  • Distinguish between criticism of practices versus targeting adherents
  • Understand context when sharing religious content
  • Be especially careful on social media platforms
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can establish lack of intent to cause communal disharmony, demonstrate the academic or good faith nature of communications, and contest overbroad interpretations of speech regulations.

Protests & Unlawful Assembly

Participating in protests without permission can violate Section 144 CrPC, potentially leading to detention and charges.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Seek proper permissions before organizing gatherings
  • Understand where and when assembly prohibitions are in effect
  • Follow instructions of authorities during protests
  • Avoid activities that could escalate tensions
  • Know your rights regarding peaceful assembly
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can challenge improper detentions, establish the peaceful nature of gatherings, defend against charges of rioting or unlawful assembly, and seek permissions for legitimate protests.

Free Speech & Social Media Laws ‚û°

Religious & Public Order

×

Sedition Charges

Criticizing the government in a way that incites violence can lead to charges under Section 124A IPC, though this law has been increasingly scrutinized.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Understand the distinction between criticism and incitement
  • Be aware of the constitutional protections for speech
  • Maintain records of exact statements made publicly
  • Contextualize criticism within democratic discourse
  • Avoid calls for violent opposition to authorities
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can challenge sedition charges based on constitutional protections, establish that criticism falls within legitimate democratic discourse, and highlight recent Supreme Court limitations on sedition prosecutions.

Contempt of Court

Criticizing or disrespecting the judiciary can lead to contempt proceedings, fines, and potential imprisonment.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Distinguish between criticism of judgments versus impugning motives
  • Be factually accurate when discussing court proceedings
  • Avoid statements suggesting courts acted with bias
  • Understand that ongoing cases require particular care
  • Frame criticism within academic or reform contexts
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can help frame apologies where appropriate, establish good faith in statements, demonstrate that comments were fair criticism within democratic norms, and appeal contempt findings.

Miscellaneous Legal Issues ‚û°

Religious & Public Order

×

Voting Requirements

Government employees are required to vote in elections, and failure to do so can result in disciplinary action.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Maintain updated voter registration
  • Apply for postal ballots if unable to vote in person
  • Document valid reasons for inability to vote
  • Understand leave policies for voting days
  • Keep evidence of having voted (like inked finger photos)
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can help establish valid reasons for non-voting, address departmental inquiries, and ensure proportional responses to voting failures.

Document Updates

Issues with passport, Aadhaar, PAN, or voter ID due to incorrect information can lead to service denials and legal complications.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Regularly verify information on identification documents
  • Update address and other details promptly when changed
  • Maintain consistent information across all documents
  • Keep copies of all identification documents secure
  • Follow proper procedures for corrections rather than creating new IDs
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can help navigate bureaucratic processes for corrections, address service denials due to document discrepancies, and resolve identity verification challenges.

Document Falsification

Providing false information in official documents like affidavits or government forms can lead to prosecution for perjury or fraud.

How to Safeguard Yourself
  • Verify all information before submitting official documents
  • Understand the legal significance of signing affidavits
  • Seek assistance for complex forms if needed
  • Disclose information fully when legally required
  • Keep evidence of good faith efforts to provide accurate information
What an Advocate Can Do

An advocate can establish lack of intent to defraud, show good faith errors rather than deliberate falsification, negotiate for opportunities to correct information, and minimize penalties for inadvertent mistakes.
